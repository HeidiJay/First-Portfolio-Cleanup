To learn about a computer's hardware I thought one of the best ways was some hands on experience. With my husbands help, we unassembled and reassembled both my laptop and a broken laptop. We also tried to upgrade the RAM's. We got to see and touch each component throughout the internal hardware of the 2 HP laptops.

After reassembly I got to learn what loud beeping noises mean coming from the computer. The number of deeps represent a targeted problem in the computer's hardware. My laptop had 2 consecutive beeps, representing a problem with the RAM I installed. I found out that each RAM labeled with pairs of numbers and the pairs basically indicates the speed of the RAM. The number paired after DDR refers to the number of megatransfers (MT) per second. The number paired after PC refers to the theoretical bandwidth in megabytes per second.

The image above shows the RAM I had (4gb) and the one I was trying to use to replace it(16gb). As you can see above, I had the wrong type of RAM. So I got to unassembled and reassemble my laptop again to replace the new RAM with the old RAM. Afterwards the computer worked fine and the deeping stopped.

In the question of "What is Memory vs Storage?". Memory refers to the location of short-term data, storage is the component of your computer that allows you to store and access data on a long-term basis. Usually, storage comes in the form of a solid-state drive or a hard drive.

Operating System is the software that supports a computer's basic functions, such as scheduling tasks, executing applications, and controlling peripherals.

There are many types of Operating Systems, but I have experience in two, Windows and Linux. Before I started to educated myself in technology, I had made the mistake of falling into a scam and allowing a hacker onto my computer who was claiming to be from a popular Virus cleaning company called Norton. After I started education myself about computers, I followed a YouTube tutorial to clean off my hard drive and installed Linux Mint onto my computer, thus ellimating the hacker access problem. Thus started my adventure in learning and using my new operating system, my Linux. I have loved the experience.
